PRO files editing on some example

by Maltanar


Q : Let's say I want to use an existing animation the stoneskin thingy (that rock rises from the ground and shatters when the stoneskin spell is cast - I think it's also used for some petrify spell..not sure)...let's say I don't want  to make an Earth themed elemental spell..let's call it Earth Strike (could also be a monk innate ability) I want the rock animation to travel in a line toward the target and end with another graphic on impact...just how hard would it be to pull this off?

And what exactly are the steps to take? 

A : Well, VVC files simply put positioned graphics and sound effects together, so they won't be much use for you. The "Earth Strike" graphic, as I understand, is a ball of earth/ a rock traveling in the air, then striking the target with another impact graphic, right? If so, you need a new PRO file for the projectile graphic, meaning this post will soon go into the Other File Type Editing . I am assuming you don't want it to cause wide area of effect.

Take these steps:
-Create a BAM of rock or earth swirling or spinning in place, the BAM which will be display upon impact, and if you wish, its shadow in another BAM. 
-Use Infinity Projectile Utility to create a projectile with these parameters:
Projectile Type: Single Target
Projectile Speed: (whatever you want)
Sound Resource: (again, you may leave it blank or assign a WAVC name)
Graphic Resource: The name of the BAM you have created
Shadow Resource: If you have, the name of the shadow BAM you created.
Display: Original BAM
Color: (blank)
Click on Create Projectile, then name the new projectile EARTHBL
-Make the spell with your damage/etc. parameters in IEEP, set type to Projectile, leave the Graphic blank, and add a Play 3D Effect, with the BAM resource as the impact BAM you have created.
-Reopen IPU, click on Change Item/Spell animation, open the spell file you have created, select Extension Header 1, scroll down in the projectile list on the right, select EARTHBL, and click on Change...it's done.

Q : For the play 3d effect, do I choose pre-target or self? 

A : Pre-Target, as it's going to be played on the target upon impact.
